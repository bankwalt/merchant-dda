Business Savings Account Disclosure

Partner SMB Savings Position

Effective: April 16, 2026 | v 1.1 (Draft — Pending Compliance & Legal Review)

PURPOSE OF THIS DISCLOSURE

This Business Savings Account Disclosure (this “Disclosure”) describes the terms under which interest is earned and fees are assessed on the Partner Savings position (the “Savings Account” or “Account”) offered to small and medium-sized business Merchants through the Partner platform. This Disclosure supplements the Partner Deposit Account Terms & Conditions, which govern the underlying deposit relationship. In the event of a conflict with respect to rate, yield, or fee information specific to the Savings Account, this Disclosure controls.

ACCOUNT DESCRIPTION

The Partner Savings Account is an interest-bearing deposit position held within the same Merchant Platform Account as your Partner Demand Deposit Account (“DDA”). The Savings Account is designed to allow Merchants to earn interest on funds held within the Partner platform without requiring transfers to an external bank. The Savings Account is a business deposit product and is available only to Merchants with an active Partner DDA.

Funds held in the Savings Account are insured by the FDIC through the applicable Partner Bank, subject to aggregation with other deposits held at the same Partner Bank in the same ownership category. FDIC coverage is described further in the Partner Deposit Account Terms & Conditions.

ACCOUNT TERMS

ACCRUAL, COMPOUNDING, AND CREDITING

Interest accrues daily on the end-of-day ledger balance of the Savings Account using the daily balance method, which applies a periodic rate to the balance in the Account at the end of each day, provided the end-of-day ledger balance meets the minimum balance required to earn interest. Accrued interest is compounded monthly and credited to the Savings Account on a monthly basis, typically by the 10th of the following month.

Interest accrues only on days when the end-of-day ledger balance is at or above $50.00. On any day when the end-of-day ledger balance is below $50.00, no interest accrues for that day. When the balance subsequently meets or exceeds $50.00, interest begins to accrue again on the full balance for each qualifying day.

Interest begins to accrue on the business day Partner receives provisional credit for deposits into the Savings Account, subject to the minimum balance requirement above. If the Savings Account is closed before interest is credited for a period, any interest accrued but not yet credited for that period will be forfeited.

FEES

The Savings Account has no monthly maintenance fee and no minimum balance fee. Fees applicable to transactions involving the Savings Account (for example, fees associated with transfers to or from the DDA that are processed through external rails) are set forth in the Partner Fee Schedule.

TRANSFERS AND WITHDRAWALS

Transfers between the Partner DDA and the Savings Account are processed as internal book transfers within the same Merchant Platform Account. Internal transfers post in real time and are reflected immediately in the Account balances, subject to any operational or fraud-prevention reviews.

The Savings Account is an internal deposit position and does not support direct external transfers, external ACH debits, wire transfers, or card transactions. To move funds out of the Savings Account to an external destination, the Merchant must first transfer funds from the Savings Account to the DDA, from which external transfers may be initiated subject to the terms applicable to the DDA.

Partner may apply operational limits on the frequency or amount of transfers from the Savings Account to the DDA for fraud prevention, risk management, or compliance purposes. Current limits, if any, are published within the Partner platform and may be updated from time to time.

FDIC INSURANCE

Savings Account balances are insured by the FDIC through the Partner Bank holding the Merchant Platform Account, up to applicable limits. Because the Savings Account is held within the same Merchant Platform Account as the DDA, balances in the Savings Account are aggregated with the DDA (and any other deposits the Merchant holds at the same Partner Bank in the same ownership category) for purposes of calculating FDIC coverage. The current FDIC coverage limit is $250,000 per depositor, per FDIC-insured bank, per account ownership category.

If the combined balance across the DDA, Savings Account, and any other deposits at the same Partner Bank in the same ownership category exceeds $250,000, the portion above that limit is not FDIC-insured and could be lost in the event of Partner Bank failure.

RELATIONSHIP TO OTHER JARIS AGREEMENTS

This Disclosure is specific to the Partner Savings Account. General terms governing the deposit relationship (including liability, legal process, dispute resolution, and amendments) are set forth in the Partner Deposit Account Terms & Conditions. Information collection, use, and disclosure are governed by the Partner Merchant Privacy Policy. In the event of a conflict between this Disclosure and the Partner Deposit Account Terms & Conditions with respect to rate, yield, or fee information specific to the Savings Account, this Disclosure controls.

CHANGES TO THIS DISCLOSURE

Partner may update this Disclosure from time to time, including to reflect changes in the Interest Rate, APY, or other terms. Updates will be posted through the Partner platform with a revised effective date. Changes to the Interest Rate and APY resulting from changes to the underlying Federal Funds Target Rate do not require advance notice. Material changes to other terms will be provided with reasonable notice as required by applicable law and the Partner Deposit Account Terms & Conditions.
